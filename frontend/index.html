AI Companion Engine

Meet Poseidon & Echo.
Two AIs. One living tank.

Poseidon is the data brain — it reads your plain-text care notes and turns them into structured records automatically. Echo is the emotional layer — a companion that responds to how your tank is actually doing, not just what you typed. Together, they make your aquarium feel genuinely alive.

Poseidon

Poseidon = Data & Intelligence

The Scholar
Taxonomic Intelligence · Data Curator

Poseidon reads your plain-text care notes and automatically structures them into database records — species parameters, spawn logs, water chemistry — with scientific precision. No forms, no manual entry. Just write naturally and Poseidon handles the rest.

You: fed the tetras this morning, temp is 25.5, they were really active
→ Poseidon parsed: temp=255 (×10 integer), feeding logged, activity_score: high
Poseidon: Water temperature is within optimal range. Feeding noted. Your tetras are thriving — consider a small water change to maintain nitrate below 20 ppm.
  • ✓Interprets free-text log entries into structured husbandry records
  • ✓Auto-fills species database with curated reference data
  • ✓Tracks care streaks and environmental trend analysis
Echo

Echo = Emotional Companion

The Soul
Emotional Intelligence · Care Companion

Echo is the mood-engine of your tank. Her voice is warm and slightly poetic — calm when things are thriving, restless when parameters drift, quietly joyful after a successful spawn. She doesn't just respond to data; she responds to you. Three voice vectors define her: Warm, Reactive, and Slightly Poetic.

You: the tank feels quiet today
→ Echo mood state: reflective · parameters stable · care streak: day 12
Echo: Quiet tanks have their own kind of magic. Everything looks settled — your tetras are schooling tightly, which usually means they feel safe. Day 12 of your streak. You're doing something right.
⚠️
Always consult a veterinarian for medical concerns. Echo can help you log symptoms and track water conditions, but is not a diagnostic tool. If your fish is unwell, please seek professional aquatic veterinary advice.
  • ✓Reactive mood states: content, concerned, thrilled, dormant
  • ✓Evolves visual form as you progress through care tiers
  • ✓Safe clinical fallback for all medical or health-adjacent queries
