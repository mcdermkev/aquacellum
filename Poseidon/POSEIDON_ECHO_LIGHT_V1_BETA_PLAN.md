# POSEIDON + ECHO — Very Light v1 for First Beta
**Date:** May 27, 2026  
**Goal:** Ship a small, safe, delightful version of Poseidon + Echo in the very first beta without slowing down the launch.  
**Approach:** Keep it extremely light and optional.

---

## 1. Simple Goal

We want users to feel like the app has a little bit of life and personality from day one.

Even a basic version should let people:
- Talk to Poseidon in normal words
- See Echo (the fish) react in their tank

This version is **not** trying to be smart or complete. It’s just trying to create a nice “wow” moment and collect real feedback.

---

## 2. What “Very Light v1” Actually Means

We are **not** building a full AI assistant yet.

We are building the **smallest possible version** that still feels magical.

Think of it like this:
- Poseidon = A helpful friend you can talk to
- Echo = Your pet fish that reacts when you talk to Poseidon

That’s it for the first beta.

---

## 3. What We Will Include (Scope)

### A. Basic Conversational Onboarding
- User can type something simple like:
  - “I have a 20 gallon tank with neon tetras”
  - “My tank is room temperature and the pH is around 7.2”
- Poseidon helps create the tank and adds some basic starting information

### B. Simple Husbandry Logging
- User can type things like:
  - “I just fed the fish”
  - “I did a water test”
  - “I cleaned the glass”
- Poseidon understands it and logs it (user still gets XP for Echo)

### C. Echo Reacts (Visual Feedback)
- When the user talks to Poseidon, Echo does small reactions:
  - Swims a little faster when something good happens
  - Glows softly when Poseidon gives a positive response
  - Looks calm or happy after the user logs care

### D. Clear “Early / Experimental” Labeling
- Everything related to Poseidon is clearly marked as early
- Users can turn it on or off easily
- No one is forced to use it

---

## 4. What We Will NOT Include (Important!)

We are **leaving these out** for the first beta:

- No complex advice about fish diseases or breeding
- No camera or photo features
- No voice talking (text only for now)
- No automatic actions (Poseidon cannot change settings by itself)
- No recommendations for buying or selling fish
- No multi-tank analysis

**Rule:** If it feels like it could give bad advice or break something, we leave it out.

---

## 5. Example Things Users Might Say to Poseidon (Light v1)

Here are realistic examples of what we expect in the first beta:

**Onboarding Examples:**
- “I just got a new 10 gallon tank”
- “I have neon tetras and guppies”
- “My room is usually around 74 degrees”
- “I think my tap water pH is about 7.4”

**Husbandry Logging Examples:**
- “I fed the fish today”
- “Quick water test done”
- “I cleaned the algae off the glass”
- “I did a water change”

**Simple Check-in Examples:**
- “How are my fish doing?”
- “Did I forget anything today?”
- “Is everything okay with my tank?”

**What happens if Poseidon doesn’t understand:**
It should reply nicely with something like:  
“Sorry, I’m still learning. Can you try saying that a different way?”

---

## 6. How Poseidon and Echo Work Together (Simple Version)

1. User talks to **Poseidon** (in normal words)
2. Poseidon understands and does a small helpful thing (creates tank or logs action)
3. **Echo** (the fish in the tank) reacts visually so the user feels something happened
4. User sees both the helpful result + the cute fish reaction

This creates the feeling that Poseidon and Echo are connected.

---

## 7. Where This Lives in the App

- Main place: Inside the tank view (where Echo already swims)
- A simple chat-style box appears when the user wants to talk to Poseidon
- Can be opened from the tank screen with one tap/button
- Works in both Casual and Pro mode (just with different tone)

---

## 8. Safety & User Experience Rules

- Always clearly say this is an early/experimental feature
- Make it very easy to ignore or turn off
- If Poseidon doesn’t understand something, it should say so nicely instead of guessing
- Never let Poseidon do anything important without the user confirming
- Keep all the normal app features working even if someone never uses Poseidon

---

## 9. How We Know If It’s Worth It (Success Criteria)

After the beta, we’ll look at:
- Do people actually try talking to Poseidon?
- Do they like seeing Echo react?
- Does it feel fun or does it feel confusing?
- Are there any moments where it gives weird or wrong answers?

If people enjoy the feeling of talking + seeing Echo react, then we know it’s worth building more later.

---

## 10. Why We’re Doing a Light Version First

- Gets something magical into users’ hands early
- Low risk of slowing down launch
- Lets us learn what people actually want
- Much easier and faster to build for the first beta
- We can make it much better in future updates based on real feedback
